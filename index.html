Aristotle and the Greeks and their beliefs

On 360 BC I remember the day the Greece born Aristotle proved his theory about how all planets are spherical. It was a great day for Astronomy. Back then, Aristotle believed in the Geocentric model of the universe. He thought that earth was being revolved around by everything. According to him, the Sun, planets, and stars were located in spheres that revolved around the Earth.

More information on Aristotle

Ptolemy and his beliefs

I remember Ptolemy well, he was a great man. I met him in egypt, his homeland, around 150 AD. Ptolemy came up with the geocentric model. The model that states the Earth is the center of the universe. He also believed that the circle in which planets theoretically revolved around us, was a perfect circle. We can understand from common day knowledge that this theory was false. But good old Ptolemy believed greatly in his theory so he created the first actual model with the Earth as the center of the universe.
Biography on Ptolemy

Copernicus and his beliefs

Now Copernicus was one smart man from Poland. He didn't think that it was logical for the earth to be in the center of the universe. Instead he thought that maybe the planets revolved around the sun. At first it did not seem realistic because everyone was stuck on the idea of a geocentric universe. So when Copernicus proposed the idea for the helio centric model,people thought he was crazy. It was only after many years to come did people not think of him as insane. Which was about around the year 1512.

Biography on Copernicus

Tycho Brahe and his contributions.

Tycho Brahe made a very important contribution to Astronomy. It was because of him that people started to believe in the heliocentric model. Now Tycho was definitely passionate about his feelings toward studying the cosmos. He lost his nose in a sword fight with his third cousin over a mathematical formula. So, he glued many precious metals to his face to make up for his lost nose. The king of Sweden knew about Tycho's love for astronomy so he granted Tycho and island where he could conduct his studies further. During 1580 is when Tycho started to get more and more astronomers to come to his island for research. It was a beautiful island. I remember it well.

Discoveries of Tycho Brahe

Galileo and his contributions and findings

In 1609, Galileo perfected the first ever telescope. This allowed people to view the stars clearer and closer. Telescopes have evolved greatly since back then but we all owe it to the genius mind of galileo for making it happen.

Johannes Kepler and his contributions

Johannes was a smart man. Because of him, we are able to understand planetary motion better. 1619 was when he finished his three laws of planetary motion. (1) the planets move in elliptical orbits with the Sun at one focus; (2) the time necessary to traverse any arc of a planetary orbit is proportional to the area of the sector between the central body and that arc (the “area law”); and (3) there is an exact relationship between the squares of the planet's’ periodic times and the cubes of the radii of their orbits (the “harmonic law”). -Kepler.

During July 1942, the first rocket was launched into space from Germany called the V2. This changed space exploration forever. It was a monumental leap for mankind the day that the first rocket was launched because we finally knew that we could actually go into space and explore more. America then started to build rockets to compete with the german ones. They wanted to have the best technology. This also started a new chapter in the world of physics.

The passengers were the dogs Belka and Strelka, plus a gray rabbit, 42 mice, two rats and fruit flies." They were used to test if humans could survive in space. On April 1961, Yuri Gagarin became the first human to travel into space. He was originally in the air force and because of this he was elected to be a cosmonaut. He indeed was the one chosen for the job and made history as he stepped into that rocket.

1st American in Space

On may 5, 1961, Alan B. Shepard was the first american to make it to space and back. This was only 23 days after the first man, Yuri Gagarin, went into space. This was called Project Mercury and it was one of the three projects set to get to the moon.

JFK Speech and the creation of NASA

On May 25, 1961, President John F. Kennedy announced the goal of sending an American safely to the Moon before the end of the decade. Everyone thought he was insane and this was impossible. America was very bad at creating rockets at the time and during that period people thought JFK was out of his mind. NASA started getting to work on a rocket that would safely bring astronauts to the moon and back. They started out with three major projects which will be explained later in the website.

The decision to go to the moon.

1st man to orbit the earth

On February 20, 1962, NASA astronaut John Glenn became the first American to orbit the Earth in his Mercury capsule Friendship 7. This was the second of the three projects to get to the moon. John Glenn circled the earth three times, reaching speeds of more than 17,000 miles per hour.

Article on John Glenn

3 projects

Projects Mercury, Gemini, and Apollo were the three main projects NASA worked on to get a man on the moon. It was after JFK's speech about reaching the moon that these projects were made. Project Mercury was completed in 1963. The goal was to get a man into earth's orbit and back safely. Project Gemini was completed in 1966 with the goal to connect two different spaceships in space. This was necessary for landing on the moon. The final step was project Apollo with the objective of landing on the moon, collecting samples and making it safely back to earth.

Information on the 3 projects

Apollo 11 and the moon landing

Now one of the greatest days for mankind was the day that Neil Armstrong stepped foot on the moon. It was him, Edwin "Buzz" Aldrin and Michael Collins that went up in the rocket together. This day on July 20, 1969, America changed the world. This was eight years after JFK made his decade goal of putting a man on the moon. As Neil stepped on the moon he said these famous words. "One small step for man, one giant leap for mankind".

Later Apollo missions

The last and final Apollo mission was the Apollo 17 mission which was the 6th moon landing and the ninth manned mission beyond low earth orbit. The reason the Apollo missions stopped was because NASA simply did not have the time or money to continue them. One of the most dangerous Apollo missions was Apollo 13 where there was an explosion in one of the oxygen tanks that forced the crew to come back to earth without landing on the moon. Annual Solar Eclipse

The ring of fire, the main phase of this annular solar eclipse, was visible along a path from the southern tip of South America to Angola in Africa. In the surrounding areas, people saw a partial solar eclipse. There is also a rumor that states if you look directly into the eclipse you will go blind. America's last solar eclipse was on August 21, 2017.
